    100 STUDENTS TRAINED AS 2021 GIRLS-IN-ICT PARTICIPANTS

    The Girls-in-ICT Trainer of Trainers (ToT) kick-started in Sefwi-Wiawso Municipality in the Western-North Region on August 17, 2021. A total of 100 teachers benefitted from the training as part of the bi-annual National Girls-in-ICT celebrations. The teachers in turn trained 1,000 girls from 10 selected districts in the region namely Sefwi Wiawso, Juaboso, Bodi, Akontombra, Suaman, and Bibiani Awianso Bekwai, Aowim, Bia East, Bia West, Prestea Huni-Valley. As part of the training, a brief tour and inspection of the training centres was carried out by representatives of the Ministry and GIFEC, an agency under MoCD. Opening the training, Mr. Selorm Dzokoto, the Regional Manager for GIFEC noted, "Girls-in-ICT is a programme organised to train girls in ICT, especially in coding, to whip up their interest in the current ICT world". He added that girls who performed well in the training would receive certificates and awards for the establishment of ultramodern ICT laboratories in their schools, to provide more room for the schools to advance their skills in ICT.The Sefwi-Wiawso Municipal Chief Executive, Mr. Louis Owusu Agyapong, advised the trainers to take the training seriously since it represented good opportunity to grow their knowledge base and help improve the job skills of the girls they were to train. The training of the girls took place from August 30 to September 10; and the district competitions was from September 4 to 11, with the climax on September 15, 2021. The awards were as follows: All 1,000 girls were awarded with certificates of participation. The schools attended by the top 10 girls were given ICT laboratories. The top 100 girls were given laptops; and the top three girls got cash prices and plaques. In attendance were the District Education Director, Mr. Stephen Kwaku Owusu, representatives from the Kofi Annan Centre of Excellence, GIFEC and MOCD.


    The MoCD Minister, Mrs. Ursula Owusu-Ekuful, on June 1, 2021 paid a working visit to the management and staff of the Accra Digital Centre (ADC), an Agency under the Ministry. The Minister's visit was to get a date on the Agency's activities, plans, challenges and intention to expand the Centre's infrastructure to train more young people in the country in ICT. Speaking at the meeting, the Managing Director of ADC, Mr. Kofi Ofosu Kansah, said the Centre has a core mandate to provide a conducive environment for digital innovators and entrepreneurs. For that reason, it supports three various clusters ... Business Process Outsourcing (BPO), which refers to the process of contracting standard business functions to be handled by a party outside of the company; Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs); and Start Ups - where with the help of the Minister, under the e-Transform Programme, government has been able to set up Innovation Hub to help in the development of technology-based companies with high growth potential of Tech businesses.
    The MD noted that the Centre had achieved almost all the performance indicators set for them in 2017. "The Centre has put over 10,000 youth in various digital schools," he said. Touching on the challenges, Mr Ofosu Nkansah said the ADC is not able to generate enough funds internally to sustain the operations of the Centre and to do capital projects. He entreated the Minister to help the Centre get more funds to rehabilitate their dilapidated infrastructures and fix outmoded office equipment in various offices across the country. The Minister commended the ADC for their work and asked them to engage better with the Ministry and other stakeholders to help them move to the next level in their field of work. The Minister also encouraged the staff to write bespoke proposals and develop programmes to get more partners for the sector to attract more funding. She urged Management and staff to use social media handles to advertise their activities to the public to get more partnerships from both within and outside the country. In attendance were the then Deputy Minister designate for MOCD, Ms. Ama Pomaa Boateng, Management and staff of Accra Digital Centre and some representatives from the Ministry.
